Our Services

Comprehensive services designed to support youth working through trauma, chemical dependency, and behavioral issues on their journey toward independence and success.

Group Home Services

24/7 Structured Care

Our 16-bed licensed Group Home A operates around the clock, providing supportive housing for youth working through trauma, chemical dependency, behavioral issues, and other challenges:

  • Daily structure and supervision
  • Strengths-based level system
  • Personalized treatment planning
  • Three meals a day plus snacks
  • Individual, group, and family therapy
  • Weekly progress reviews and goal-setting
  • Home visit planning and aftercare coordination

Point & Level System

Our structured behavioral programming rewards positive choices and growth:

  • Following directions the first time
  • Respect for staff and peers
  • Positive participation in therapy and school
  • Use of coping skills during challenges

Youth earn privileges through progress, including outings, calls, recreation, and eventually home passes.

Key Behavioral Goals We Track

Accepting feedback
Managing anger
Communicating emotions
Making safe decisions

Education Program

Onsite Classroom

Education is a core part of our day. All youth attend school onsite in our fully accredited classroom led by certified special education teachers and paraprofessionals.

We partner with local school districts, including Fillmore Central Public Schools, to ensure continuity and credit transfer.

Educational Services

  • Individualized learning plans
  • GED preparation (if applicable)
  • Credit recovery support
  • Special education services
  • Transition-to-work readiness
  • FAFSA assistance for college-bound students

Mental Health & Substance Use Support

Therapeutic Services

Our licensed therapist provides comprehensive mental health support:

  • Weekly individual therapy sessions
  • Group therapy and psychoeducation
  • Family therapy and involvement
  • Crisis intervention and support

Evidence-Based Approaches

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) - Helping youth identify and change negative thought patterns
  • Substance Use Psychoeducation - Education and support for substance-related issues
  • Trauma Processing - Specialized trauma-informed therapeutic interventions
  • Social Skills Training - Building interpersonal and communication skills
  • Emotional Regulation - Learning healthy coping strategies

Dual-Diagnosis Support

For youth with dual-diagnosis needs, we coordinate with community providers for integrated care and medication management when needed.

A Day in the Life

From breakfast to lights out, our youth follow a predictable, nurturing schedule that builds life habits and emotional safety.

Time Activity Focus
7:00 AM Wake Up & Morning Hygiene Personal responsibility and self-care
7:30 AM Breakfast Nutrition and community dining
8:00 AM Chores & Room Check Life skills and accountability
8:30 AM Classroom Learning Begins Academic progress and engagement
12:00 PM Lunch Nutrition and social interaction
1:00 PM Afternoon School Session Continued learning and academic work
3:00 PM Recreation / Free Time / Additional Chores Physical activity and life skills
4:00 PM Quiet Time, Journaling, and Reflection Reflection and emotional processing
5:00 PM Dinner Family-style meals and conversation
6:00 PM Free Time / Family Phone Calls / Game Time Social connection and recreation
8:00 PM Group Session or Snack Therapeutic work and community building
After Group/Snack Room Time, Showers, Lights Out Rest and preparation for tomorrow
