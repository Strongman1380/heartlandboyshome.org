About Heartland Boys Home

Learn about our mission, philosophy, and commitment to empowering youth through trauma-informed care.

Who We Are

Our Foundation

Heartland Boys Home is a trauma-informed, structured Group Home A located in Geneva, Nebraska, dedicated to housing male youth ages 12â€“18 who are working through trauma, chemical dependency, behavioral issues, and other challenging life circumstances.

Whether a young man is coming from the juvenile justice system, foster care, or experiencing family instability, Heartland offers a safe, caring, and consistent environment designed to help them heal, grow, and succeed.

Our Credentials

We are licensed as a Group Home A and operate with full compliance under Nebraska's Residential Child-Caring Agency (RCCA) regulations, providing a supportive home environment for youth working through complex challenges.

Our facility is guided by evidence-based practices and staffed with experienced professionals committed to empowering youth through structure, education, emotional support, and positive reinforcement.

Our Mission

At Heartland Boys Home, our mission is to stabilize youth, support families, and build bridges to independence. We focus on helping young men understand their worth, learn life skills, manage emotions, and prepare for successful transitions back to home, school, and community.

Our Philosophy

We believe that every young person deserves dignity, safety, and a second chance. Our approach is built on four core principles:

  • Every young person deserves dignity, safety, and a second chance
  • Change happens through relationships, consistency, and support
  • Healing requires addressing trauma, not just behavior
  • Youth thrive in environments where expectations are clear, and accountability is balanced with compassion

Why Heartland Works

Long-term Growth Focus

Unlike short-term detention or institutional placements, Heartland emphasizes long-term growth. Our graduates leave with:

Emotional Skills

Improved emotional regulation and coping strategies that help youth manage stress and challenges in healthy ways.

Stronger Relationships

Better relationships with family members, mentors, and peers built on trust, communication, and mutual respect.

Decision-Making

Enhanced decision-making skills and the ability to think through consequences before acting.

Educational Progress

Educational credits, diploma progress, and readiness for further education or career training.

Future Planning

Real, actionable plans for work, school, or transition services that set them up for success.

Life Skills

Practical life skills including financial literacy, job readiness, and independent living capabilities.

Behavioral Goals of Placement

Our structured approach focuses on measurable outcomes that prepare youth for successful transitions and long-term positive change.

Behavior Stabilization and Emotional Regulation

Developing healthy coping mechanisms and emotional awareness to manage stress, anger, and challenging situations effectively.

Social Skill Development

Building positive relationships, communication skills, and the ability to work cooperatively with peers and adults.

Decision-Making and Personal Responsibility

Learning to think through consequences, make thoughtful choices, and take ownership of actions and their outcomes.

Building Internal Motivation

Developing self-driven goals, personal values, and the internal drive to pursue positive outcomes and personal growth.

Developing Independence and Life Skills

Mastering practical skills needed for independent living, including self-care, financial responsibility, and daily life management.

Reducing Recidivism and Risk Behaviors

Breaking cycles of negative behavior through skill-building, awareness, and the development of healthy alternatives to risky choices.

Our Location

Geneva, Nebraska

Located in the heart of Nebraska, Geneva provides a safe, small-town environment that supports healing and growth. Our facility offers:

  • 16-bed Group Home A capacity
  • Fully equipped educational classroom
  • Therapy and counseling spaces
  • Recreation and common areas
  • 24/7 supervised environment

Safe Environment

Our facility is designed with safety and therapeutic value in mind. We maintain:

  • Secure but non-institutional setting
  • Trauma-informed physical spaces
  • Areas for both group and individual activities
  • Comfortable living spaces that feel like home
  • Professional supervision at all times
